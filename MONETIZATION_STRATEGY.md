## üí≥ Pricing Psychology

### Why These Price Points?

**Free Tier ($0)**
- Generous enough to be useful
- Limited enough to drive conversions
- 50 recipes = ~2-3 months of regular use before hitting limit
- Creates invested users who don't want to lose progress

**Starter Tier ($4.99/month, $39.99/year)**
- **Sweet spot**: Most users will choose this
- Less than 2 coffees/month
- Justifiable for regular home cooks
- Annual = $3.33/month (great deal)
- **Target: 60-70% of paid users**

**Pro Tier ($9.99/month, $79.99/year)**
- 2x Starter price for 3x value
- Premium positioning
- Annual = $6.67/month
- For serious users and families
- **Target: 30-40% of paid users**

### Anchoring Strategy
- Show annual pricing first (makes monthly seem expensive)
- Highlight savings: "Save $20/year" badge
- Default selection: Annual Starter (highest perceived value)

## üîÑ Upgrade/Downgrade Policies

### Upgrade (Easy & Instant)
- ‚úÖ Immediate access to new tier features
- ‚úÖ Prorated pricing (pay difference for current month)
- ‚úÖ Keep all existing data
- ‚úÖ One-click upgrade in Settings

### Downgrade (User-Friendly)
- ‚úÖ Takes effect at end of billing cycle
- ‚úÖ Keep data (read-only access to excess)
  - Example: Downgrade from Starter to Free with 100 recipes
  - Keep all 100 recipes (read-only)
  - Can't add new recipes until below 50
  - Can delete recipes to get back under limit
- ‚úÖ Grace period: 30 days to re-upgrade before hard limits
- ‚úÖ Export all data before downgrade (one-click backup)

### Cancelation (No Hard Feelings)
- ‚úÖ Cancel anytime
- ‚úÖ Access until end of paid period
- ‚úÖ Export all data before account downgrades
- ‚úÖ Re-subscribe anytime with data intact (stored for 12 months)
- ‚úÖ Exit survey: "What could we improve?"
